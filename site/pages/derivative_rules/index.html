Chain, Product And Quotient Rules

Some while ago remade my GLSL automatic differentiation library (GitHub repository) to include some more functions. Turns out I didn't write down the Jacobians and Hessians in the vector/matrix form that I need. Also turns out that I could not find them anywhere on the internet! So I had to redo them and decided to write them down this time, just in case... maybe someone else might be interested in them.

The next will summarize the results. Derivations can be found below. Derivatives are taken with respect to xxx, though I will leave out the ()()() parameter brackets, if they are not needed to make things clear. Gradients (∇\nabla∇), Jacobians (JJJ) and Hessians (HHH) will apply to the object on the right. Brackets are used, if any of them apply to multiple terms.

If you find an error, feel free to tell me.

Summary

Chain Rules

Derivative rules for compositions of functions (f∘g)(x)=f(g(x))(f \circ g)(x) = f(g(x))(f∘g)(x)=f(g(x))

Gradient Chain Rule

Scalar form

f:R→Rg:Rn→R \begin{aligned} f &: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ g &: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ \end{aligned} fg​:R→R:Rn→R​

∇f(g)=f′∇g\nabla f(g) = f'\nabla g ∇f(g)=f′∇g

Multi parameter form

f:Rk→Rg:Rn→Rk \begin{aligned} f &: \mathbb{R}^k \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ g &: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^k \\ \end{aligned} fg​:Rk→R:Rn→Rk​

∇f(g)=(Jg)T∇f\nabla f(g) = (Jg)^T\nabla f ∇f(g)=(Jg)T∇f

Jacobian Chain Rule

f:Rm→Rkg:Rn→Rm \begin{aligned} f &: \mathbb{R}^m \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^k \\ g &: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^m \end{aligned} fg​:Rm→Rk:Rn→Rm​

J(f∘g)∈Rk×nJ(f∘g)=Jf⏟∈Rk×mJg⏟∈Rm×n \begin{aligned} J({f \circ g}) &\in \mathbb{R}^{k \times n} \\ J({f \circ g}) &= \underbrace{J{f}}_{\in \mathbb{R}^{k\times m}}\underbrace{Jg}_{\in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}} \end{aligned} J(f∘g)J(f∘g)​∈Rk×n=∈Rk×mJf​​∈Rm×nJg​​​

Hessian Chain Rule

Scalar form

f:R→Rg:Rn→R \begin{aligned} f &: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ g &: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \end{aligned} fg​:R→R:Rn→R​

H(f∘g)∈Rn×nH(f∘g)=f′′∇g∇Tg+f′Hg \begin{aligned} H({f\circ g}) & \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n} \\ H({f\circ g}) &= f''\nabla g \nabla^T g + f'Hg \end{aligned} H(f∘g)H(f∘g)​∈Rn×n=f′′∇g∇Tg+f′Hg​

f:Rk→Rg:Rn→Rk \begin{aligned} f &: \mathbb{R}^k \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ g &: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^k \end{aligned} fg​:Rk→R:Rn→Rk​

Derivation Gradient Chain Rule

Scalar form:

This is listed in most places, but I put it in because it is used in the other derivations. It is easy to check by looking at a partial derivative and using the usual one dimensional chain rule.

∂f(g)∂xi=f′∂g∂xi \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial f(g)}{\partial x^i} &= f'\frac{\partial g}{\partial x^i} \end{aligned}∂xi∂f(g)​​=f′∂xi∂g​​

These are exactly the entries of the gradient.

Multi parameter form:

Can be seen from index notation as well or as a special case of the Jacobian Chain Rule, when k=1 k=1k=1 and using ∇f=(Jf)T \nabla f = (Jf)^T∇f=(Jf)T for a scalar function.

Derivation Gradient Product Rule

This is listed in most places, but I put it in because it is used in the other derivations. It is easy to check by looking at a partial derivative and using the usual one dimensional product rule.

∂fg∂xi=g∂f∂xi+f∂g∂xi \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial fg}{\partial x^i} &= g\frac{\partial f}{\partial x^i} + f\frac{\partial g}{\partial x^i} \end{aligned}∂xi∂fg​​=g∂xi∂f​+f∂xi∂g​​

These are exactly the entries of the gradient.

Derivation Gradient Quotient Rule

Use the product and chain rule for gradients.

fg=fg−1=fq \begin{aligned} \frac{f}{g} &= fg^{-1} \\ &= fq \end{aligned}gf​​=fg−1=fq​

Derivatives of qqq

q(g)=g−1q′=−1g2q′′=2g3 \begin{aligned} q(g) &= g^{-1}\\ q' &= -\frac{1}{g^2}\\ q'' &= \frac{2}{g^3} \end{aligned}q(g)q′q′′​=g−1=−g21​=g32​​

∇fg=∇(fq(g))=q(g)∇f+f∇q(g)=q(g)∇f+f(q(g)′∇g)=1g∇f−fg2∇g=g∇f−f∇gg2 \begin{aligned} \nabla\frac{f}{g} &= \nabla (fq(g)) \\ &= q(g)\nabla f + f\nabla q(g) \\ &= q(g)\nabla f + f (q(g)'\nabla g) \\ &= \frac{1}{g} \nabla f - \frac{f}{g^2}\nabla g \\ &= \frac{g\nabla f - f\nabla g}{g^2} \end{aligned}∇gf​​=∇(fq(g))=q(g)∇f+f∇q(g)=q(g)∇f+f(q(g)′∇g)=g1​∇f−g2f​∇g=g2g∇f−f∇g​​

Derivation Jacobian Product Rule

The elements of AAA are ai,ja_{i,j}ai,j​ and the elements of bbb are bib_ibi​. The matrix product is then just ci=ai,jbjc_i = a_{i,j}b_jci​=ai,j​bj​. The Jacobian elements are J(c)i,k=∂ci∂xkJ(c)_{i,k} = \frac{\partial c_i}{\partial x^k}J(c)i,k​=∂xk∂ci​​.

J(c)i,k=∂ci∂xk=∂ai,jbj∂xk=bj∂ai,j∂xk+ai,j∂bj∂xk=bj∂ai,j∂xk+ai,jJ(b)j,k \begin{aligned} J(c)_{i,k} &= \frac{\partial c_i}{\partial x^k} \\ &= \frac{\partial a_{i,j}b_j}{\partial x^k} \\ &= b_j\frac{\partial a_{i,j}}{\partial x^k} + a_{i,j}\frac{\partial b_j}{\partial x^k}\\ &= b_j\frac{\partial a_{i,j}}{\partial x^k} +a_{i,j}J(b)_{j,k} \end{aligned} J(c)i,k​​=∂xk∂ci​​=∂xk∂ai,j​bj​​=bj​∂xk∂ai,j​​+ai,j​∂xk∂bj​​=bj​∂xk∂ai,j​​+ai,j​J(b)j,k​​

A scalar function aaa can be represented as a scaled identity matrix ai,j=aδija_{i,j}= a\delta_i^jai,j​=aδij​ leading to

J(c)i,k=bj∂aδij∂xk+aδijJ(b)j,k=bi∂a∂xk+aJ(b)i,k \begin{aligned} J(c)_{i,k} &= b_j\frac{\partial a\delta_i^j}{\partial x^k} +a\delta_i^jJ(b)_{j,k} \\ &= b_i\frac{\partial a}{\partial x^k} + aJ(b)_{i,k} \end{aligned}J(c)i,k​​=bj​∂xk∂aδij​​+aδij​J(b)j,k​=bi​∂xk∂a​+aJ(b)i,k​​

Or back in matrix notation:

J(ab)=aJb+b∇TaJ({ab}) = aJb + b\nabla^Ta J(ab)=aJb+b∇Ta

Hf(g)=J∇f(g)∇f(g)=f′∇g \begin{aligned} Hf(g) &= J\nabla f(g)\\ \nabla f(g) &= f'\nabla g \end{aligned}Hf(g)∇f(g)​=J∇f(g)=f′∇g​

From the Jacobian product rule it follows that:

Hf(g)=J(f′∇g)=f′J∇g+∇g∇T(f′)=f′Hg+∇g∇T(f′)=f′Hg+∇gf′′∇Tg=f′Hg+f′′∇g∇Tg \begin{aligned} H{f(g)} &= J({f' \nabla g}) \\ &= f'J{\nabla g} + \nabla g\nabla^T(f')\\ &= f'Hg + \nabla g\nabla^T(f') \\ &= f'Hg + \nabla g f''\nabla^Tg \\ &= f'Hg + f'' \nabla g \nabla^Tg \end{aligned}Hf(g)​=J(f′∇g)=f′J∇g+∇g∇T(f′)=f′Hg+∇g∇T(f′)=f′Hg+∇gf′′∇Tg=f′Hg+f′′∇g∇Tg​

Derivation Hessian Product Rule

Apply the gradient product rule and the additive property of derivatives.

H(fg)=J∇(fg)∇(fg)=f∇g+g∇fJ(f∇g+g∇f)=J(f∇g)+J(g∇f) \begin{aligned} H({fg}) &= J{\nabla(fg)} \\ \nabla(fg) &= f\nabla g + g\nabla f\\ J({f\nabla g + g\nabla f}) &=J({f\nabla g}) + J({g\nabla f}) \end{aligned}H(fg)∇(fg)J(f∇g+g∇f)​=J∇(fg)=f∇g+g∇f=J(f∇g)+J(g∇f)​

Differentiating both Jacobians with the Jacobian product rule.

J(f∇g)=fJ∇g+∇g∇Tf=fHg+∇g∇TfJ(g∇f)=gHf+∇f∇Tg \begin{aligned} J({f\nabla g}) &= fJ{\nabla g} + \nabla g\nabla^Tf \\ &= fHg + \nabla g\nabla^Tf\\ J({g\nabla f}) &= gHf+ \nabla f\nabla^Tg \end{aligned}J(f∇g)J(g∇f)​=fJ∇g+∇g∇Tf=fHg+∇g∇Tf=gHf+∇f∇Tg​

Putting these together gives the final result

H(fg)=J(f∇g)+J(g∇f)=fHg+∇g∇Tf+gHf+∇f∇Tg=fHg+gHf+∇g∇Tf+∇f∇Tg \begin{aligned} H({fg}) &= J({f\nabla g}) + J({g\nabla f})\\ &= fHg + \nabla g\nabla^Tf + gHf+ \nabla f\nabla^Tg\\ &= fHg + gHf + \nabla g \nabla^T f + \nabla f \nabla^T g \end{aligned}H(fg)​=J(f∇g)+J(g∇f)=fHg+∇g∇Tf+gHf+∇f∇Tg=fHg+gHf+∇g∇Tf+∇f∇Tg​

Derivation Hessian Quotient Rule

Start similar to the product rule, but use q(g)=1gq(g) = \frac{1}{g}q(g)=g1​. The derivatives of qqq are listed in the gradient quotient rule derivation.

Hfg=H(fq)=J(f∇q)+J(q∇f) \begin{aligned} H{\frac{f}{g}} &= H({fq}) \\ &= J({f\nabla q}) + J({q\nabla f}) \end{aligned}Hgf​​=H(fq)=J(f∇q)+J(q∇f)​

The first term:

J(f∇q)=fJ∇q+∇q∇Tf=fHq+∇q∇Tf \begin{aligned} J({f\nabla q}) &= fJ{\nabla q} + \nabla q\nabla^Tf\\ &= fHq+ \nabla q\nabla^Tf \end{aligned}J(f∇q)​=fJ∇q+∇q∇Tf=fHq+∇q∇Tf​

Compute ∇q\nabla q∇q with the gradient chain rule

∇q(g)=q′∇g=−∇gg2 \begin{aligned} \nabla q(g) &= q'\nabla g \\ &= -\frac{\nabla g}{g^2} \end{aligned}∇q(g)​=q′∇g=−g2∇g​​

Use the hessian chain rule

Hq=q′′∇g∇Tg+q′Hg=2∇g∇Tgg3−Hgg2 \begin{aligned} Hq &= q''\nabla g \nabla^T g + q'Hg \\ &= \frac{2\nabla g \nabla^T g}{g^3} - \frac{Hg}{g^2} \end{aligned}Hq​=q′′∇g∇Tg+q′Hg=g32∇g∇Tg​−g2Hg​​

The second term

J(q∇f)=qHf+∇f∇Tq=Hfg−∇f∇Tgg2 \begin{aligned} J({q\nabla f}) &= qHf+ \nabla f\nabla^Tq \\ &= \frac{Hf}{g} - \frac{\nabla f \nabla^T g}{g^2} \end{aligned}J(q∇f)​=qHf+∇f∇Tq=gHf​−g2∇f∇Tg​​

Putting things together:

Hfg=J(f∇q)+J(q∇f)=fHq+∇q∇Tf+Hfg−∇f∇Tgg2=f(2∇g∇Tgg3−Hgg2)−∇g∇Tfg2+Hfg−∇f∇Tgg2=2f∇g∇Tgg3−fHgg2−∇g∇Tfg2+Hfg−∇f∇Tgg2=2f∇g∇Tgg3−fHg+∇g∇Tf+∇f∇Tgg2+Hfg \begin{aligned} H{\frac{f}{g}} &= J({f\nabla q}) + J({q\nabla f}) \\ &= fHq+ \nabla q\nabla^Tf + \frac{Hf}{g} - \frac{\nabla f \nabla^T g}{g^2} \\ &= f (\frac{2\nabla g \nabla^T g}{g^3} - \frac{Hg}{g^2}) - \frac{\nabla g \nabla^T f}{g^2} + \frac{Hf}{g} - \frac{\nabla f \nabla^T g}{g^2}\\ &= \frac{2f\nabla g \nabla^T g}{g^3} - \frac{fHg}{g^2} - \frac{\nabla g \nabla^T f}{g^2} + \frac{Hf}{g} - \frac{\nabla f \nabla^T g}{g^2}\\ &= \frac{2f\nabla g \nabla^T g}{g^3} - \frac{fHg + \nabla g \nabla^T f + \nabla f \nabla^T g}{g^2} + \frac{Hf}{g} \end{aligned}Hgf​​=J(f∇q)+J(q∇f)=fHq+∇q∇Tf+gHf​−g2∇f∇Tg​=f(g32∇g∇Tg​−g2Hg​)−g2∇g∇Tf​+gHf​−g2∇f∇Tg​=g32f∇g∇Tg​−g2fHg​−g2∇g∇Tf​+gHf​−g2∇f∇Tg​=g32f∇g∇Tg​−g2fHg+∇g∇Tf+∇f∇Tg​+gHf​​
